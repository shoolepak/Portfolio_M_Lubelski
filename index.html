Horrors of the Mundane

The horrors of the mundane. The man could not stop thinking about it.
We do this every day. We pick up the same objects, use them for the same purposes, carry out the same activities while we have the same kinds of thoughts‚Äîor lack thereof actually. The emptiness in the mind must come, else, there would be suffering, there would be torture. There would be pain.
It was not a bad day, a little tiring perhaps. The thoughts came either way. Swooped in like vultures to peck at, and rip the flesh out. The flesh of the dying creature, but creature not yet fully deceased.

And so he pondered, glancing at the object. A well kept object, but still, surrounded with filth. Placed in a glass, a glass probably never washed, with residue of ambiguous origin and nature. The translucency of the glass, the intended objective to show the liquid contents clear and true, perverted. That is perhaps an overstatement, as the container was simply repurposed, it‚Äôs new destiny was to hold a singular object: An ordinary toothbrush.

Somehow it never occurred to him how filthy was the glass he kept his toothbrush in. And he was not a dirty person by any means, or so he believed himself to be. Do normal people wash their toothbrush glasses regularly? Like, we all know about the brush itself‚Äîthe thing you put in your mouth‚Äîbut the glass you hold it in? Do people even use regular glasses for that? Important questions to ask yourself, no doubt about that. Maybe even a certain urge began to develop, an urge for him to clean the glass‚Äîone tormenting thought would be gone this way‚Ä¶

That made him go back. Back to the damn vultures, the annoying little thinklets circling around him. Wouldn‚Äôt it be nice, to just be a cloud? Clouds don‚Äôt need to brush their teeth, wipe their asses, go to work, impress anyone. They don‚Äôt care if anybody loves them. They just float about, get carried by the wind. Not a care in the world.
Not a care in the world.
Oh it is a privilege to be able to think. However, to have to think, is sometimes incredibly tiring. It was not a bad day, but even on good days he could ponder on how hopeless it all may seem at times. What was our direction? What was the purpose of it all? Why had he been brushing his teeth multiple times a day, just to have them develop a likeness for rot?

Dental caries though, are not his only worry. Other than the overwhelming concern with purpose of the human civilisation and consciousness itself, there‚Äôs more pressing matters. There‚Äôs the divorce, the ownership rights, the dreadful Patrick, and that cute girl Annie, who seems to somewhat like him. That is the trouble though. Anything else, he can withstand right now, but love and rotten teeth seem like a bit too much for the time being.

He stood there, barefoot, his mind somewhere far away, racing with all the different birds. The vultures, the eagles, the seagulls, penguins, pigeons and ostriches. One of the birds must have hit a glass window, a dirty window, window murky due to Limescale. That‚Äôs it, He remembered. Limescale. Harmless residue found in tap water, mineral water, any water not purified, not distilled. The thought made him calmer, ready to move on, ready to stop staring. There is no escape from it anymore. He stood there long enough. The mundane must continue, the life must go on. It is time to pick up the brush.

Over the years, I‚Äôve experimented with other software like Maya, ZBrush, and Cinema 4D. The models and textures I created were often imported into various games and engines, including Source, Source 2, Unity, and Unreal.

About

Marek Lubelski, Multidisciplinary creator

Hi, my name is Marek Lubelski, but you can call me Mark!

I believe I always loved to create, and that is why after graduating High School, I enrolled at AUB, which is short for Arts University Bournemouth.

While studying there, I chose a program called Foundation Diploma in Art and Design, and as my specialisation, I chose 3D Animation. The course lasted for one year, and I finished it with the highest possible grade, but as everyone knows, it's not the grades that matter the most, but the abilities we gain.

While studying at AUB, I gained (and improved) a lot of abilities, in the fields of:

  • 3D animation
  • Traditional animation
  • Stop motion animation
  • Graphic design
  • Video editing
  • Sound design

After finishing the Foundation year, I travelled a lot, worked in various places around the world and further developed my abilities on my own. In the meantime, I got into:

  • Writing
  • 3D modelling and texturing
  • Logo design
  • Video editing
  • Board game design

To this day, Digital art in all forms remains my core interest, and that is why I am currently studying at the Leuphana University of L√ºneburg, with Digital Media as my major.

While Studying at Leuphana, I also had an opportunity to participate in an exchange at University of Guelph-Humber in Toronto, where I learned web design, and got introduced into VR app development.

My biggest dream is to work in the Video Game industry, as I am largely passionate about digital worlds, love to both play and create video games, and things for them, such as mods.
